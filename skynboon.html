A Vancouver based furniture carpenter wanted to expand their business with a simple ecom storefront. With limited programming knowledge they wanted the freedom to alter the design once it was up and running.

The Backbone

A CMS was the obvious choice for its ease-of-use. I'd worked with Drupal, Joomla, Ghost, and a number of other management services in the past, but in the end decided to go with Wordpress for two main of reasons.

  • User base - the Wordpress ecosystem is enormous, four times the size of it's nearest competitor. With that comes easier-to-find tutorials, plug-ins, themes, and developers for the client once the product is in their hands.
  • Ease of use - other CMS (like Droopal and Joomla) take more programming knowledge to get going.

The Front

Once wordpress was chosen I had to build a custom theme and, along with that, a test page that could be used as a reference to see how all the pieces interact.

Then I wrote up this custom widget so the client can keep the front page updated with whatever products they want to showcase. Download the widget off my github if you want to use it for your own projects.
